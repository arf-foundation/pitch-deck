🧠 Bayesian risk scoring – expected loss minimisation

E[L] = P(Failure | Action) × Impact(cost) + β × Regret P(Fail) = posterior from operational memory; Impact = $ exposure; Regret = what agent would lose if it acted
  • P(Failure | Action) : conjugate priors + HMC + hyperpriors (ARF core engine v4)
  • Impact : dollar exposure, SLO violation, regulatory fine
  • β × Regret : optional counterfactual regret term
  • Output: Approve · Deny · Escalate — deterministic, signed
[3:30-5:30] Explain formula simply – it’s not about being right, it’s about expected cost. The regret term is novel: we penalise actions that would have been worse than doing nothing.
