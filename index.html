Problem Epistemic risk

AI systems fail in ways traditional software does not.

  • Agents act under uncertainty, then compound their own errors.
  • Failures are often silent until impact is visible downstream.
  • Telemetry exists, but it is rarely aligned to decision quality.
  • Reactive monitoring sees symptoms after the system has already drifted.
In autonomous systems, the core question is not whether failure occurs. It is whether failure is observable, containable, and recoverable.
Non-stationarity
Hidden state
Cascading error
Weak feedback loops
The failure mode is structural: decision-making is probabilistic, but production expectations are deterministic.

How it works Mathematical framing

ARF formalizes reliability as a decision problem under uncertainty.

risk_score ≈ uncertainty × impact × propagation × time_to_detection

if risk_score > threshold:
  trigger remediation()
else:
  continue_with_observability()
This is a practical control heuristic, not a claim of perfect prediction. The goal is to reduce expected harm by acting earlier on weaker signals.
  • Signal aggregation converts fragmented telemetry into a usable state estimate.
  • Risk scoring prioritizes likely failure before hard failure manifests.
  • The remediation policy is explicit, inspectable, and versionable.
  • A feedback loop updates memory so future decisions are sharper.
P(state | evidence) → risk estimate → policy action → new evidence
