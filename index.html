🧠 Bayesian risk engine – how it works (high level)

  • Online learning: Every outcome (success/failure) updates the risk estimate for that action category – fast, lightweight.
  • Hierarchical memory: Sparse categories borrow strength from the global average (no cold‑start problem).
  • Offline deep model: Trained on historical patterns (time of day, user role, environment) to catch subtle risks.
  • Weighted fusion: The final risk intelligently blends all three sources based on how much data is available – more data = more weight on the deep model.
  • Calibrated confidence: The engine is continuously tested against real outcomes (54/54 pressure tests) to ensure a 1% predicted risk really means 1% failure rate.
