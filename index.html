📖 Glossary – demystifying the jargon

  • Bayesian inference: A method of updating the probability of a hypothesis as more evidence becomes available.
  • Conjugate prior: A prior distribution that, when combined with the likelihood, yields a posterior of the same family – simplifies computation.
  • HMC (Hamiltonian Monte Carlo): A Markov chain Monte Carlo method for sampling from complex probability distributions.
  • RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation): Using a vector database to retrieve relevant past incidents to inform the current decision.
  • FAISS: Facebook AI Similarity Search – library for efficient similarity search and clustering of dense vectors.
  • Deterministic policy: Given the same input, the same output is guaranteed – no randomness.
